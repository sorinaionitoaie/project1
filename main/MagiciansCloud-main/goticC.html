Intro

  Gothic architecture is a style that flourished in Europe in during the Middle Ages High and late. It evolved from Romanesque architecture and was succeeded by architecture Renaissance. One among the most emblematic characteristic of Gothic architecture is the pointed arch.

Famous Gothic cathedrals
Characteristics

  Gothic churches are known for their tall windows, pointed, vaults with ribs and buttresses. These architectural elements allowed for greater height and natural light inside the buildings.
